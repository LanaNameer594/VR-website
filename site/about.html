About Us

In this immersive world, users interact with their surroundings in a completely natural way using their hands and gaze to engage with the magical elements around them. Imagine opening your palm to welcome a canary that lands on your hand, or touching colorful mushrooms that respond with melodic sounds as butterflies dance their way to lead you deeper in the forest. Every interaction in Magical Forest VR is designed to spark curiosity and wonder, encouraging players to explore and discover the enchanting ecosystem in which they find themselves. It’s a place where magic feels real, and every moment is filled with possibility.

Through these enchanting encounters, the experience gently guides users toward a deeper understanding of their connection to the planet and their power to protect it.

The Storyline

The primary goal of Magical Forest VR is to engage young people through immersive storytelling that captures their imagination and inspires them to take action for the planet. The journey begins by inviting users to explore the forest, allowing them to connect with its beauty and feel a sense of presence. However, this magical world soon reveals a troubling reality: the forest is in danger of dying. As players venture deeper into the forest, they are guided by a wise canary and other mystical creatures who subtly reveal that something is not quite right.

Players eventually encounter a mischievous goblin hidden in the shadows, hoarding resources and carelessly wasting electricity, water, and food. At first, the goblin appears comical and strange—but as the narrative unfolds, its behavior becomes disturbingly familiar. The forest begins to wither, and players witness the impact of the goblin’s actions on the environment around them.

The story reaches a powerful climax when the goblin vanishes into a lake, leaving behind ripples of decay. As players look into the water, they see not just the goblin’s reflection—but their own. In this pivotal moment of realization, the story turns inward: the goblin is not a distant villain, but a reflection of our own unsustainable behaviors.
